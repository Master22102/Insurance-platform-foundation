## 1. Purpose & legal basis

GDPR Article 17 and UK GDPR equivalents grant data subjects the right to
request erasure of personal data. This protocol defines how requests are
received and verified, which data is erased, which data is lawfully
retained, how the append-only event ledger is reconciled with erasure
obligations, and how erasure is logged without creating a paradox.

### The fundamental tension ŌĆö resolved

The append-only immutable event ledger is a core architectural invariant.
GDPR Article 17 requires erasure. Resolution: the ledger records that
events happened (event types, actor IDs, scope IDs), while the personal
data associated with those events (names, documents, descriptions) lives
in domain tables that are erasable. Ledger rows can have PII-containing
JSONB fields redacted in place while preserving chain integrity. **The
ledger proves the platform behaved. It does not need to contain the
traveler's passport number to do so.**

## 2. Scope

Applies to any natural person whose personal data is processed: account
holders, group participants, emergency contacts, and any person whose
data appears in uploaded evidence or claim documentation.

Does not apply to: corporate-entity data (legal names, registered
addresses), anonymized / aggregated data, or data already anonymized via
routine retention expiry.

## 3. Request intake & verification

### Channels
- **In-platform**: Privacy & Data Controls surface (Section 7.3) with a
  "Request data erasure" action.
- **Email**: designated privacy contact address (e.g.,
  `privacy@wayfarer.com`).
- **Support**: support staff MUST route to Founder within 24 hours. They
  do not action erasure directly.

### Identity verification
- Authenticated (in-platform): session authentication suffices; request
  tied to the authenticated account.
- Unauthenticated (email): requestor provides (a) email address of
  record, (b) one verifiable account detail (trip name, signup date);
  step-up OTP to registered email confirms identity before proceeding.
- Minor / incapacitated: guardian or legal representative provides
  documentation of authority; Founder reviews before proceeding.

### Response timeline
- **Acknowledgment**: within 72 hours of receipt.
- **Completion**: within 30 days of verified request (Article 12).
- **Extension**: complexity-based extension up to 2 additional months
  (total 3 months); subject notified within the initial 30-day window
  with explanation.

## 5. Per-object PII field register

Section 8.9.3 defines which fields are erased vs retained per object.
The table is normative; implementation reads directly from the doctrine.
Summary of object coverage:

- `Trip` ŌĆö erase `trip_name`, `destination_summary`; retain trip_id,
  financial and ledger metadata.
- `Policy` / `PolicyVersion` ŌĆö erase `policy_label`, `provider_name`,
  `raw_artifact_path` (file deleted from storage), coverage/deductible/
  exclusion JSONs; retain policy_id, content_hash, confidence_tier.
- `Incident` ŌĆö erase `title`, `description`; retain id, trip_id,
  canonical_status, disruption_type, causality_status.
- `Evidence` ŌĆö erase `description`, `file_path` (file deleted),
  `name`; retain id, hash_sha256.
- `Claim` ŌĆö erase `denial_reason_text`, `primary_provider` if
  personally identifying; retain claim_id, status, financial outcomes.
- `Contact` ŌĆö erase `display_name`, `phone_number`, `email`,
  `relationship`, `notes`; retain contact_id, contact_type.
- `ParticipantChecklistItem` ŌĆö erase `item_detail`, `resolution_notes`;
  retain item_id, trip_id, status.
- `ScanJob` ŌĆö erase `feature_flag_snapshot` if contains PII; retain
  scan_id, credits.
- `ScanCreditLedger` ŌĆö no PII fields; all retained.
- `InterpretiveTraceRecord` ŌĆö erase `input_context`, `raw_output` if
  contains PII; retain trace_id, decision_fingerprint, confidence_label.
- `EventLedger` ŌĆö redact PII within `previous_state` / `resulting_state`
  JSONB; preserve event_id chain, event_type, timestamps, scope_ids,
  anonymized actor_id.

## 6. Event ledger redaction protocol

Append-only: no rows deleted. Mechanism:

1. Identify all `event_ledger` rows where `previous_state` or
   `resulting_state` contains PII for the subject.
2. For each row, write a row to `erasure_redaction_log` with
   `event_id`, `erasure_record_id`, `field_path`, `original_hash`
   (SHA-256 of the original value ŌĆö proves field existed without
   reproducing it), `pre_redaction_checksum`, `post_redaction_checksum`,
   `redacted_at`, `redacted_by`.
3. Replace PII field values with `"[ERASED ŌĆö erasure_record_id: {id}]"`.
4. Recompute checksum per row. Log both pre- and post-redaction
   checksums.
5. `event_id` chain, `event_type`, timestamps, `scope_id` never
   modified.

### Actor anonymization

When an account is deleted, `event_ledger.actor_id` is replaced with a
stable anonymous identifier: a UUID derived from a one-way hash of
`(original account_id, platform_secret)`. Consistent across all rows
for the same original actor so replay and causality analysis remain
intact. "This action was taken by someone" preserved; "that someone was
Christian Doe" not.

## 7. Lawful retention exceptions (Article 17(3))

| Exception | Retained | Duration | Disclosure |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Legal obligation ŌĆö financial records | Claim outcomes, payment records, credit ledger | 7 years from transaction | "Financial transaction records are retained for 7 years per legal requirement. These contain no personally identifying information beyond what is required for the financial record." |
| Legal obligation ŌĆö audit trail | Event ledger (redacted per ┬¦6), ITR decision fingerprints | 7 years from event | "Audit trail records are retained to demonstrate platform behavior. Personal data within these records has been erased. The records prove we acted correctly ŌĆö they do not reproduce your personal information." |
| Defence of legal claims ŌĆö dispute hold | Evidence / documents subject to an active dispute | Until dispute resolution + 90 days | "One or more documents are subject to an active dispute hold and cannot be erased until the dispute closes. You will be notified when these are erased." |
| Legal hold | Data subject to a lawful hold order | Duration of hold | "Certain data is subject to a legal hold. We cannot provide details of the hold." |

## 8. Nine-step execution sequence

| # | Action | Event emitted |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Verify identity, validate request, check dispute & legal holds | `pii_erasure_requested` |
| 2 | Generate `erasure_record_id`; create row | `pii_erasure_initiated` |
| 3 | Delete T1/T2 objects from storage (passport images, policy documents, evidence files); confirm | `pii_erasure_storage_purged` |
| 4 | Null / erase PII fields in domain tables per ┬¦5 | `pii_erasure_domain_tables_processed` |
| 5 | Execute ledger redaction per ┬¦6; recompute checksums; write `erasure_redaction_log` | `pii_erasure_ledger_redacted` |
| 6 | Anonymize `actor_id` in ledger per ┬¦6 | `pii_erasure_actor_anonymized` |
| 7 | Mark all ITR records for the account `pii_redacted = true` | `pii_erasure_itr_flagged` |
| 8 | Document dispute-hold / legal-hold exceptions | `pii_erasure_exceptions_recorded` |
| 9 | Generate confirmation document (what was erased, what was retained, legal basis, completion date, `erasure_record_id`); deliver to subject | `pii_erasure_completed`, `pii_erasure_confirmation_delivered` |

Every event carries the `erasure_record_id` so a full trace can be
assembled from the ledger alone.

## 9. Invariants (E-I-01 ŌĆ” E-I-09)

- **E-I-01** ŌĆö 30-day processing window enforced; extensions notified
  and reasoned within the initial window.
- **E-I-02** ŌĆö Event ledger never directly deleted. PII redacted via
  `erasure_redaction_log`; chain integrity preserved.
- **E-I-03** ŌĆö Every request has a unique `erasure_record_id`; it
  appears on every emitted event and in the confirmation document.
- **E-I-04** ŌĆö Storage deletions are confirmed and logged; a confirmation
  token is stored on the erasure record; Founder is alerted on failure.
- **E-I-05** ŌĆö Retained data is explicitly disclosed to the subject.
- **E-I-06** ŌĆö Dispute holds and legal holds are documented at erasure
  time; when a hold lifts, deferred erasure proceeds automatically and
  the subject is notified.
- **E-I-07** ŌĆö No justification required from the subject; identity
  verification only.
- **E-I-08** ŌĆö Erasure of one account does not erase data about that
  person held in another account's records (e.g., emergency contact).
- **E-I-09** ŌĆö Protocol is reviewed annually; the per-object field
  register in Section 8.9.3 updated whenever any Section 3.2 object
  gains a new PII field.

## 10. RPCs (SECURITY DEFINER, search_path = '')

- `open_erasure_request(p_scope_descriptor jsonb, p_identity_method text)` ŌĆö
  inserts a row, emits `pii_erasure_requested`.
- `verify_erasure_request(p_erasure_record_id uuid)` ŌĆö sets
  `verified_at`, status ŌåÆ `verified`.
- `execute_erasure_step(p_erasure_record_id uuid, p_step int)` ŌĆö runs
  one of the nine steps idempotently, emits the corresponding event,
  advances status.
- `record_hold_exception(p_erasure_record_id uuid, p_hold_type text,
  p_until timestamptz, p_reference text)` ŌĆö records dispute / legal
  hold against the request.
- `complete_erasure_request(p_erasure_record_id uuid)` ŌĆö final
  transition; generates confirmation document; emits
  `pii_erasure_completed` and `pii_erasure_confirmation_delivered`.

All RPCs require service role for state mutation; authenticated role
may call `open_erasure_request` and read their own request history.

## 14. Acceptance criteria

- [ ] `erasure_requests` table exists with the schema above and RLS.
- [ ] All ten `pii_erasure_*` event types registered with feature_id
      F-6.8.9.
- [ ] Unauthenticated email path requires OTP step-up; session path
      accepts auth.uid().
- [ ] Nine execution steps are idempotent; re-running a completed step
      emits no new ledger rows and returns success.
- [ ] Ledger redaction preserves `event_id` chain; pre- and
      post-redaction checksums both stored.
- [ ] Actor anonymization: same original actor_id always maps to the
      same anonymous UUID across rows.
- [ ] Dispute and legal hold exceptions defer the affected items and
      automatically resume erasure when the hold lifts.
- [ ] Confirmation document enumerates what was erased, what was
      retained, and the legal basis for retention.
- [ ] RLS enforcement: requestor cannot read another user's requests;
      only privacy admin role sees the queue.
